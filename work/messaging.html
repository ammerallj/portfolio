Creating Continuity Across Messaging

Designing the interaction model that brought messaging back into Facebook as private sharing expanded.

This work shipped to millions of people across Facebook, but the design artifacts and case study remain confidential.

Overview

On Facebook, sharing something privately is how conversations start—a Reel, a post, or a Marketplace listing sent to one person. But the reply happened in Messenger, and the moment happened somewhere else.

Meta was reporting more than 140 billion messages a day across its apps. As messaging expanded inside Facebook, independently designed entry points created fragmented interaction patterns—making conversations less predictable for people and harder to extend consistently across new experiences.

As the lead designer on this initiative, I partnered with engineering, content design, research, and data science to define a reusable interaction model for Facebook's in-app messaging—a predictable way for people to enter and continue conversations without being redirected to Messenger. The model kept guiding implementation after handoff.

Impact

  • Standardized in-app messaging across Facebook surfaces by designing a reusable interaction model that continued guiding rollout after handoff.
  • Informed a rollout addressing ~144M monthly switches from Facebook to Messenger, advancing Facebook's strategy to bring private sharing back into the core app.
  • Validated the interaction model through experimentation: +21.9% thread opens (iOS), +27.1% (Android), while maintaining neutral topline metrics.
