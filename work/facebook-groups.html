Helping People Navigate Communities

A sense of place across many communities.

Timeline
2022–2023
Role
Product Designer · Interaction Design · Design Systems · Design Quality
Team
2 Product Designers · Content Design · Product Management · Engineering · Research

Overview

Most people on Facebook belong to at least 15 active groups, and more than 100 million people join one every day. At that scale, Groups had become a sprawl of communities, roles, and spaces, and people needed clearer ways to understand where they were and move between them without losing context.

I redesigned navigation, transitions, states, and core interaction patterns across Community Home and key entry points. I designed inviter-card and panel patterns, drove design-system consistency across iOS and Android, and used cross-surface audits and prototypes to identify quality gaps and strengthen shared patterns.

Impact

  • Moved topline engagement on a mature consumer surface—roughly a quarter-percent lift—through refined navigation, transitions, and interaction patterns across Community Home and key entry points.
  • Raised design-system adoption across Facebook Groups to more than 80%, improving consistency across iOS and Android while reducing recurring design debt.
  • Raised the quality bar across core Groups surfaces, defining a design-system-compliant panel architecture, partnering with engineering across 17 implementation reviews, and identifying more than 200 UI quality issues.
